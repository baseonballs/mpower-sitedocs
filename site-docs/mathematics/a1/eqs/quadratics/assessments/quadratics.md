### Exercies - Forms & Structures of Quadratics

These exercises focuses on the structure and forms of quadratics, aimed at testing the identification of substructures and their characteristics:

1. **Given the quadratic equation \( f(x) = 5x^2 - 20x + 12 \), identify the following:**
    - The leading coefficient, the linear term, and the constant term.
    - Is the quadratic in standard form or does it need to be rearranged?
    - Is the graph of this quadratic concave up or concave down? Explain your reasoning.

2. **Consider the polynomial expression \( g(x) = (3x - 4)(x + 2) \):**
    - Expand the expression into a quadratic.
    - Identify the values of \( a \), \( b \), and \( c \) from the expanded form.
    - Determine if the factored form reveals the roots. What are they?

3. **For the quadratic function \( h(x) = x^2 + kx + m \):**
    - How does changing the sign of \( k \) affect the symmetry of the parabola?
    - What does \( k = 0 \) tell you about the axis of symmetry and the vertex?

4. **Analyze the quadratic \( q(x) = 4x^2 - 9 \):**
    - What substructure does this polynomial represent? (hint: difference of squares)
    - Factor the quadratic and identify its roots.
    - Is this quadratic always positive, always negative, or does it have both positive and negative values?

5. **Given the expression \( f(x) = 2(x - 1)^2 + 7 \):**
    - Convert the expression into standard quadratic form.
    - Identify the vertex of the parabola.
    - What transformations have been applied to the parent function \( x^2 \)?

6. **The quadratic equation \( p(x) = 6x^2 + 11x + 3 \) is provided:**
    - Without solving, determine the discriminant of this quadratic and describe what it tells you about the nature of the roots.
    - Is the quadratic factorable using integer values? Justify your answer.

7. **Consider the form \( f(x) = ax^2 + bx + c \):**
    - If \( a = 0 \), what does this tell you about the equation? 
    - What does this imply for the degree and shape of the graph?

8. **For the quadratic \( r(x) = x^2 + bx + 4 \), where \( b \) is unknown:**
    - Describe how to find the value of \( b \) if one of the roots is \( x = 2 \).
    - Write the quadratic in factored form once \( b \) is determined.

9. **Consider the equation \( f(x) = (x - r)(x - s) \):**
    - Expand this into standard quadratic form.
    - Explain the relationship between \( r + s \) and \( -b/a \), and \( rs \) and \( c/a \).
    - How does this form reveal both the sum and product of the roots?

10. **For the quadratic equation \( f(x) = 9x^2 + 12x + 4 \):**
    - Rewrite this quadratic in the form \( a(x - h)^2 + k \) (vertex form).
    - Determine the vertex and explain its significance for the graph.
    - Does the quadratic have a minimum or maximum value? At what point?
