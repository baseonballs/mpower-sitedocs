# **Example - Factoring a Perfect Square Trinomial**

## **Example 1: Factor Perfect Square Trinomial**:

Factor the trinomial \( x^2 + 6x + 9 \).

1. **Step 1: Recognize the form.**  
   The given trinomial \( x^2 + 6x + 9 \) fits the pattern of a **perfect square trinomial**, which has the form \( a^2 + 2ab + b^2 \). Here:

    - The coefficients are:
        -   a = 1
        -   b = 6
        -   c = 9
    - The algebraic idenity we want to use is: \( (a+b)^2 = a^2 + 2ab + b^2 \). It is known as the sum of bionomial squared
    > 
The identity \( a^2 + 2ab + b^2 = (a + b)^2 \) is called the **perfect square trinomial identity**. 
>
It represents the expansion of the **square of a binomial**, or "**square of a sum** (for short)" and it's one of the foundational identities in algebra. Specifically, it describes how to square a binomial to get a trinomial. There is a corresponding identity for the square of a binomial with a negative term:
>
>- \( a^2 + 2ab + b^2 = (a + b)^2 \) (for the positive case)
>- \( a^2 - 2ab + b^2 = (a - b)^2 \) (for the negative case)

These two identities are collectively referred to as the **perfect square identities** or **binomial square identities**.

- The variables are: x
    - \( a^2 = x^2 \) (so \( \text{the a term} = x \)),
    - \( b^2 = 9 \) (so \( \text{the b term} = 3 \)),
    - The middle term \( 2ab = 6x \) confirms that this trinomial is of the form \( (x + 3)^2 \).

2. **Step 2: Write the factorization.**  
   Since \( x^2 + 6x + 9 = (x + 3)^2 \), the factored form is:

\[
x^2 + 6x + 9 = (x + 3)(x + 3) = (x + 3)^2.
\]

Thus, perfect square trinomial \( x^2 + 6x + 9 \) factors as \( (x + 3)^2 \).

## **Example 2: Factor Perfect Square Trinomial**:

Factor \( 4x^2 - 12x + 9 \).

1. **Step 1: Recognize the form.**  
   This trinomial \( 4x^2 - 12x + 9 \) fits the form \( a^2 - 2ab + b^2 \). Here:

    - \( a^2 = 4x^2 \) (so \( a = 2x \)),
    - \( b^2 = 9 \) (so \( b = 3 \)),
    - The middle term \( -12x = -2(2x)(3) \) confirms that this is a perfect square trinomial.

2. **Step 2: Write the factorization.**

   The factored form is:

\[
4x^2 - 12x + 9 = (2x - 3)^2.
\]

## **What is a Perfect Square Trinomial**?

A **perfect square trinomial** is a trinomial that results from squaring a binomial. It takes the form of either:

- \( a^2 + 2ab + b^2 = (a + b)^2 \) (square of a sum), or
- \( a^2 - 2ab + b^2 = (a - b)^2 \) (square of a difference).

In a perfect square trinomial:
- The first term is a perfect square, \( a^2 \),
- The last term is a perfect square, \( b^2 \),
- The middle term is twice the product of the square roots of the first and last terms, \( 2ab \).

Example:

Consider the trinomial \( 4x^2 + 400x + 10000 \).

1. The first term, \( 4x^2 \), is a perfect square because \( 4x^2 = (2x)^2 \).
2. The last term, \( 10000 \), is also a perfect square because \( 10000 = 100^2 \).
3. The middle term, \( 400x \), is twice the product of \( 2x \) and \( 100 \), which confirms that it follows the form \( 2ab \).

Since \( 4x^2 + 400x + 10000 \) fits the structure of a perfect square trinomial, it can be factored as:

\[
4x^2 + 400x + 10000 = (2x + 100)^2.
\]

Thus, \( 4x^2 + 400x + 10000 \) is a perfect square trinomial.
