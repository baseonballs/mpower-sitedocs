# Exercises Forms & STructures Solutions - Solutions

## Exercise 1
**Given the quadratic equation \( f(x) = 5x^2 - 20x + 12 \):**

- **Leading Coefficient**: \( a = 5 \)
- **Linear Term**: \( b = -20 \)
- **Constant Term**: \( c = 12 \)
- **Standard Form**: The quadratic is in standard form \( ax^2 + bx + c \).
- **Concavity**: Since \( a = 5 > 0 \), the graph is concave up.

## Exercise 2
**Consider the polynomial expression \( g(x) = (3x - 4)(x + 2) \):**

- **Expansion**:

\[
g(x) = 3x^2 + 6x - 4x - 8 = 3x^2 + 2x - 8
\]

- **Values**: \( a = 3 \), \( b = 2 \), \( c = -8 \).
- **Roots**: Setting \( g(x) = 0 \):

\[
(3x - 4)(x + 2) = 0 \Rightarrow x = \frac{4}{3}, -2
\]

## Exercise 3
**For the quadratic function \( h(x) = x^2 + kx + m \):**

- **Effect of \( k \)**: Changing the sign of \( k \) reflects the graph across the y-axis.
- **\( k = 0 \)**: The axis of symmetry is \( x = 0 \), and the vertex is at \( (0, m) \).

## Exercise 4
**Analyze the quadratic \( q(x) = 4x^2 - 9 \):**

- **Substructure**: This is a difference of squares: \( q(x) = (2x - 3)(2x + 3) \).
- **Factored Form**: \( (2x - 3)(2x + 3) \).
- **Roots**: \( x = \frac{3}{2}, -\frac{3}{2} \).
- **Values**: Since the leading coefficient \( a = 4 > 0 \), the quadratic is always positive except at its roots.

## Exercise 5
**Given the expression \( f(x) = 2(x - 1)^2 + 7 \):**

- **Standard Form**:

\[
f(x) = 2(x^2 - 2x + 1) + 7 = 2x^2 - 4x + 2 + 7 = 2x^2 - 4x + 9
\]

- **Vertex**: The vertex is \( (1, 7) \).
- **Transformations**: The graph is vertically stretched by a factor of 2 and shifted right 1 unit and up 7 units.

## Exercise 6
**The quadratic equation \( p(x) = 6x^2 + 11x + 3 \):**

- **Discriminant**: 

\[
D = b^2 - 4ac = 11^2 - 4 \cdot 6 \cdot 3 = 121 - 72 = 49
\]

  Since \( D > 0 \), there are two distinct real roots.

- **Factorability**: Check for integers \( (6, 3) \) with \( 11 \):
  - Factors: \( 6x^2 + 9x + 2x + 3 = 3x(2x + 3) + 1(2x + 3) = (3x + 1)(2x + 3) \) → Yes, it is factorable.

## Exercise 7
**Consider the form \( f(x) = ax^2 + bx + c \):**

- **If \( a = 0 \)**: The equation becomes linear \( bx + c \) with a degree of 1.
- **Implication**: The graph is a straight line, not a parabola.

## Exercise 8
**For the quadratic \( r(x) = x^2 + bx + 4 \):**

- **Finding \( b \)**:
  - If one root is \( x = 2 \):

\[
r(2) = 2^2 + 2b + 4 = 0 \Rightarrow 4 + 2b + 4 = 0 \Rightarrow 2b + 8 = 0 \Rightarrow b = -4
\]

- **Quadratic**: \( r(x) = x^2 - 4x + 4 = (x - 2)^2 \).

## Exercise 9
**Consider the equation \( f(x) = (x - r)(x - s) \):**

- **Expansion**:
  \[
  f(x) = x^2 - (r + s)x + rs
  \]
- **Relationship**: 
  - \( r + s = -\frac{b}{a} \)
  - \( rs = \frac{c}{a} \)

## Exercise 10
**For the quadratic equation \( f(x) = 9x^2 + 12x + 4 \):**

- **Vertex Form**:

\[
f(x) = 9(x^2 + \frac{4}{3}x) + 4
\]

  Completing the square:

\[
= 9\left(x^2 + \frac{4}{3}x + \frac{4}{9} - \frac{4}{9}\right) + 4
= 9\left((x + \frac{2}{3})^2 - \frac{4}{9}\right) + 4
= 9(x + \frac{2}{3})^2 - 4 + 4 = 9(x + \frac{2}{3})^2
\]

- **Vertex**: \( \left(-\frac{2}{3}, 0\right) \).
- **Minimum or Maximum**: Minimum value at the vertex since \( a = 9 > 0 \).
